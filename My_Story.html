Roots in the Rural, Heart in the Horizon

My journey didn't start with a passport; it started in a small coastal village where the horizon was the only limit I knew. The rhythm of the tides taught me about patience, and the changing light of the seasons taught me about color.

When I finally left, it wasn't to escape, but to expand. I realized that every culture has a unique visual frequency—a specific way that light hits a stone wall in Provence compared to a skyscraper in São Paulo.

The First Inspiration

The Discipline of Zen

In Kyoto, I learned the power of the void. My work shifted from the decorative to the essential, focusing on the space between the strokes.

The Chaos of Color

India broke my perceptions of harmony. I found beauty in the clash of saffron and fuchsia, a sensory overload that translates to my most expressive pieces.

The Fluidity of Water

Venice taught me that nothing is static. Light is a liquid medium that reshapes the world every second. My textures mimic this constant state of flux.

Every piece has a passport. Want to see where we're going next?
